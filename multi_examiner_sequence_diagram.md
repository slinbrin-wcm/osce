# Multi-Examiner Collaborative Scoring Sequence Diagram

## Overview
This sequence diagram outlines the detailed interactions involved in the multi-examiner collaborative scoring process at exam stations. The process is divided into five phases:

1. **Preparation Phase**
2. **Scoring Phase**
3. **Review Phase**
4. **Feedback Phase**
5. **Finalization Phase**

---

## 1. Preparation Phase
- **Examiners Assignments:**
  - Assign examiners to scoring stations.
  - Provide necessary materials, guidelines, and criteria for scoring.

- **Training:**
  - Conduct training sessions for examiners on using the scoring system and understanding the evaluation criteria.
  
---

## 2. Scoring Phase
- **Scoring begins:**
  - Each examiner scores the candidates independently using a scoring platform.

- **Data Entry:**
  - Examiners input scores and remarks in real-time to ensure transparency.

- **Inter-Examiner Collaboration:**
  - Examiners can communicate during scoring via a chat feature for clarifications.

---

## 3. Review Phase
- **Score Review:**
  - After scoring, examiners review scores entered by peers for consistency.

- **Discussions:**
  - Conduct discussions to resolve any discrepancies in scoring. Use predefined metrics for evaluation.

---

## 4. Feedback Phase
- **Feedback Mechanism:**
  - Implement a mechanism for examiners to give feedback to each other regarding scoring.

- **Adjustments:**
  - Based on feedback, examiners may adjust scores if necessary and justified.

---

## 5. Finalization Phase
- **Final Scores Submission:**
  - Submit final scores after consensus is reached among all examiners.

- **Post-Assessment Review:**
  - Conduct a post-assessment evaluation of the process for continuous improvement.

- **Reporting:**
  - Generate reports summarizing the scoring process and outcomes for transparency.

---

## Closing
This sequence is vital for maintaining fairness and accountability in the scoring process, fostering a collaborative environment among examiners.  

> Ensure to follow all phases diligently to enhance the integrity of the scoring process.
